I know what you’re thinking. Actually, I don’t, to be honest. But if I were you (and presumably I’m not, because I have no good reason to read through my own posts) I would probably be thinking about three things right now:

  • Why are you changing your email address again?
  • Why are you posting about it on your blog? Is it really necessary?
  • Why am I reading my own post?

I will be using this post to attempt to answer those three questions and any questions that you might have (yes that’s right you, dear reader — one of the two that still keep my blog in their RSS feed, mostly out of the fact that removing it might inconvenience you).

So anyways, I’m here to get you started, I suppose by answer those three questions.

Q. Why are you changing your email address again?

A. To be fair, I’m not really. I plan on keeping and using me@andrewhays.net for just about everything. I like it. I’m kind of attached to it, and muddling up peoples contact books with the contact by the name of “Me” really makes my day. But it’s come to my attention that if I’m going to continue to use google services, keeping up with two accounts is going to be a pain that I don’t want to have to deal with.

So instead, I’m going to continue to use me@andrewhays.net to send and receive email, and you can continue to use it with me for gtalk, but I’m also going to start using my lesser-known gmail account drewshays@gmail.com more often, and as my primary account, so that I don’t have to switch Google accounts every five seconds. Really, this is all because of Google+, which I’ve recently started really using. All the other accounts I got used to having to make the switch whenever I’d have to check once every few days. However, now that I’ve started using Google+ more frequently, I have to switch accounts all the time. This is undesirable, obviously, and so I’m going to put a stop to it.

Q. What does this mean to me?

A. Nothing, probably. If the only way you communicate with me is through Facebook, Twitter, or Google+, feel free to keep doing everything the same way you’ve always done it. If you frequently send or receive emails to me, you can expect very little to change, although you might sometimes get an email from my drewshays@gmail.com email address. If you have me added on Google Talk, while I would prefer that you switch to using drewshays@gmail.com to communicate with me, I’m not going to do anything to stop you. I can’t guarantee that I will always be online, though (I will be with drewshays@gmail.com, on my phone), but the final decision is yours.

Q. Why are you posting about it on your blog? Is that really necessary?

A. No not really. I just like boring you.

Q. Why am I reading my own post?

A. You’re not. Unless you are, in which case I’d advise you to stop, since you’ll likely get no new information.
